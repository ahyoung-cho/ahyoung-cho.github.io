I am a Political Science PhD candidate at Boston University focusing on American politics and political methodology and I will be on the job market in 2023-2024. My research interests include environmental politics, climate justice, racial disparities, urban politics, public opinion and political methodology.

My dissertation explores how mayors perceive climate change and racial inequality and how their views are reflected in cities' climate plans. Another strand of my resesarch explores how Americans think about climate change. In particular, I am interested in understanding how racial identities shape Americans' perception of climate change.

As a political methodologist, I use various analytical techniques in my research including network, spatial, and text analysis. My co-authored and solo-authored research has been published in The Journal of Legislative Studies and JAMA Network Open.
